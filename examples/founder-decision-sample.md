# Sample Council Run

This is a synthetic example showing the shape of a successful run. It is not
saved model output.

## User Question

```text
Should we launch the product this month or cut scope first?
```

## Stage 1: Advisor Responses

- **Contrarian**: Launching now risks mistaking internal urgency for customer
  readiness. The weak assumption is that early users will tolerate unfinished
  onboarding.
- **First Principles Operator**: The binding constraint is not feature count; it
  is whether the team can observe activation, retention, and support load within
  one week.
- **Customer Realist**: A buyer will not care that the roadmap is elegant. They
  need one job done clearly, with low setup friction.
- **Expansionist**: A narrower launch can still compound if it creates a public
  proof asset and a repeatable customer story.
- **Executor**: Ship only the path needed for one buyer segment. Defer everything
  that does not prove activation or willingness to pay.

## Stage 3: Chairman Synthesis

Launch a narrower product, not the full current scope. The decision should be
framed as a learning launch with explicit kill criteria: activation rate,
support burden, and qualified customer follow-up. Cut any feature that does not
reduce buyer setup friction or improve the measurement of those signals.
